Checkout UI/UX Revamp — Reducing Friction, Increasing Trust

A research‑driven redesign of a multi‑step checkout for a mid‑market e‑commerce platform. I led the end‑to‑end UX process: discovery, IA, user flows, wireframes, prototyping, usability testing, accessibility, and delivery.

The Problem

Analytics revealed a 43% drop between shipping and payment. Recordings showed confusion around promo codes and address validation. Trust indicators were inconsistent across steps.

Constraint: Back‑end services required 3 steps; single‑page checkout was not feasible within the timeline.

Users & Context

  • Guest buyers on mobile, time‑constrained, price‑sensitive.
  • Returning buyers expecting saved addresses and one‑tap cards.
  • Edge cases: gift orders, different billing, PO boxes, cross‑border.

Research & Key Insights

We ran 10 moderated sessions and 3 rounds of unmoderated tests (n=48). Triangulated with GA funnels and 350 support tickets.

  • Trust signals: Users looked for consistent security badges and clear totals at every step.
  • Field hierarchy: Optional fields and promo code timing affected perceived complexity.
  • Error handling: Vague address errors stalled progress; inline guidance reduced retries.

UX Strategy, Information Architecture & Flows

Principles

  • Reduce cognitive load with progressive disclosure.
  • Reinforce trust: totals + badges at every step.
  • Make errors recoverable and specific.

Information Architecture

Restructured checkout into Shipping → Payment → Review with a stable order summary rail and contextual help.

User Flows

Edge cases mapped for promo codes, different billing, and gift messaging; added shortcuts for returning users.

Wireframes

Low‑fi to mid‑fi iterations validated field grouping, promo code timing, and progress feedback.

Prototype

  • Interactive prototype in Figma with realistic validation and error states.
  • Content guidelines and microcopy for sensitive inputs.
  • Motion specs for step transitions (reduced‑motion compliant).

Usability Testing

Two rounds (n=18) of task‑based tests measured time on task, error rate, and perceived effort (NASA‑TLX). Major findings:

  • Moving promo field to Review step prevented abandon during Shipping.
  • Inline address suggestions reduced error loops by 34%.
  • Persistent order summary improved trust and price recall.

Accessibility

  • WCAG 2.2 AA: focus order, visible focus, labels/ARIA for fields, error relationships.
  • Color contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for text; ≥ 3:1 for large text/UI.
  • Escalated alternative for date inputs and CVV help.

What Worked

  • Progressive disclosure and clear step framing.
  • Specific, actionable error messages with examples.
  • Consistent trust components across steps.

Next Iterations

  • Experiment with address auto‑complete providers for speed.
  • Introduce A/B for promo code entry vs. apply‑on‑review.
  • Offer express payment options on Shipping for returning users.
