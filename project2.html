Green Apple Art Center — Brand & Experience Redesign

A UI/UX redesign of Green Apple Art Center's digital experience: unified branding, a clearer student portal, and a streamlined booking flow for classes and events.

The Challenge

Brand expression was inconsistent across channels and the student portal's IA made it hard to find the right class. Mobile users faced friction during booking, with unclear pricing and policy cues reducing trust.

Constraint: The legacy CMS and back‑end needed to remain, so the new UI had to integrate without large architectural changes.

Goals

  • Unify brand across web, email, and on‑site materials.
  • Simplify the booking flow and clarify pricing/policies.
  • Improve discoverability in the student portal (search + filters).
  • Establish a scalable design system for future programs.

Research & Insights

We ran interviews with students and parents (n=12), audited analytics, and reviewed 300+ support tickets. Themes:

  • Trust markers: People wanted clear total cost and refund policy at each step.
  • Findability: Users struggled to find age‑appropriate classes via the top nav.
  • Mobile ergonomics: Dense forms and small touch targets increased abandon.

Design Solution

Design System

Introduced tokens for color/typography, a components library (cards, steps, alerts, inputs), and patterns for pricing, policies, and success states to reinforce trust.

Booking Flow

Reduced steps 8 → 5 with progressive disclosure, persistent order summary, and policy microcopy. Form fields resized and grouped for mobile ergonomics.

What Worked

  • Unified, scalable design system across touch‑points.
  • Transparent pricing and refund microcopy improved trust.
  • Mobile‑first forms reduced time on task and errors.

Next Iterations

  • Personalized dashboards for students and guardians.
  • Event discovery with saved filters and recommendations.
  • Admin analytics for enrollments and capacity planning.
